1. Vikram 2022




Watch Now

Vikram, directed by Lokesh Kanagaraj, is a high-octane action thriller that blends sleek storytelling with explosive performances. Kamal Haasan returns as the mysterious Vikram, a man with a haunting past and a mission to eliminate a drug syndicate. Vikram is joined by powerhouse performances from Vijay Sethupathi as the ruthless Santhanam and Fahadh Faasil as the sharp undercover agent Amar. The film is packed with gritty action sequences, smart twists, and subtle connections to Kaithi, expanding the Lokesh Cinematic Universe (LCU). Anirudh Ravichander’s background score is electric, adding tension and style. Vikram is a stylish, mass-loaded film with brains—perfect for fans of smart action thrillers.

2. Thuppakki 2012




Watch Now

Thuppakki, directed by A.R. Murugadoss, is a slick action thriller that showcases Vijay in top form as Jagadish, an army officer on vacation who uncovers a deadly sleeper cell plot. The film combines mass appeal with a smart narrative, featuring intense action, clever twists, and a patriotic theme that hits the mark without being preachy. Vidyut Jammwal is a formidable antagonist, and the cat-and-mouse game between him and Vijay is gripping. Harris Jayaraj’s music adds energy, and the screenplay keeps the tension alive throughout. With stylish direction and a sharp script, Thuppakki is a solid entertainer that balances intelligence, action, and emotion effectively.

3. Comali 2019




Watch Now

Comali, directed by Pradeep Ranganathan, is a fun, emotional entertainer that explores how the world changes over time. Jayam Ravi plays Ravi, a man who wakes up from a coma after 16 years and struggles to adjust to the modern, fast-paced world. The film cleverly mixes comedy, nostalgia, and social commentary, highlighting how technology has altered relationships and values. While the first half is lighthearted and packed with laughs, the second half gets more emotional and message-driven. Yogi Babu’s comic timing adds charm, and Kajal Aggarwal plays her role well. Comali isn’t perfect, but it’s a feel-good film with heart, humor, and a thoughtful message about holding onto humanity in a digital age.

4. Baahubali: The Beginning 2015




Watch Now

Baahubali 2: The Conclusion delivers an emotionally charged and visually majestic follow-up to the first film. Directed by S.S. Rajamouli, this installment answers the iconic question, “Why did Kattappa kill Baahubali?” The film dives deep into the kingdom’s politics, betrayal, and the noble heart of Amarendra Baahubali, played with grace and power by Prabhas. The storytelling is rich with drama, romance, and unforgettable moments—especially the scenes between Baahubali and Devasena. The action sequences are epic, and the visuals are even grander than the first film. M.M. Keeravani’s score uplifts every scene. Baahubali 2 isn't just a sequel—it’s a satisfying emotional payoff that cemented the saga’s legendary status.

5. Maari 2015




Watch Now

Maari, directed by Balaji Mohan, is a fun and quirky mass entertainer that revolves around Dhanush’s character, Maari, a local don with a cheeky attitude and a love for pigeons. Dhanush’s performance is full of swag, making Maari a memorable, larger-than-life character despite the film’s relatively simple plot. Kajal Aggarwal’s presence adds charm, and Robo Shankar’s comic timing as Maari’s loyal sidekick is a highlight. Anirudh Ravichander’s energetic music, especially the track “Don'u Don'u Don'u,” is one of the film’s major draws. While the film doesn’t offer much in terms of depth, it’s packed with fun and mass appeal, making it an enjoyable watch for fans of Dhanush and commercial cinema.

6. Maari 2 2018




Watch Now

Maari 2, directed by Balaji Mohan, is a thrilling and emotional sequel to Maari. Dhanush reprises his role as the lovable but fierce don Maari, and this time, the character undergoes more emotional depth and growth. The story explores themes of redemption, love, and the impact of Maari’s past. Sai Pallavi adds a lot of heart with her performance, while Tovino Thomas’s antagonist brings a solid challenge to Maari. The film balances commercial elements with genuine emotional moments, making it more engaging than its predecessor. Anirudh Ravichander’s music, especially the hit song “Rowdy Baby,” adds massive energy to the film. Overall, Maari 2 is an entertaining mix of action, humor, and emotion.

7. Geetha Govindam 2018




Watch Now

Geetha Govindam, directed by Parasuram, is a refreshing romantic comedy that strikes a perfect balance between humor, romance, and character growth. Vijay Deverakonda shines as Govind, a charming, slightly mischievous young man whose life turns upside down after an encounter with Geetha, played by Rashmika Mandanna. The film is a delightful exploration of love, misunderstandings, and redemption, with Vijay delivering a fantastic performance that mixes both humor and depth. Rashmika brings a refreshing energy to Geetha, creating a strong on-screen chemistry with Vijay. The screenplay is crisp, with relatable dialogues and a heartwarming message. The music, especially the songs "Inkem Inkem Kavale" and "What the Life," complement the film beautifully. Geetha Govindam is a feel-good movie that’s easy to fall in love with.

8. O Kadhal Kanmani 2015




Watch Now

O Kadhal Kanmani, directed by Mani Ratnam, is a beautifully crafted romantic drama that explores love in its purest form. The film follows the relationship between two young individuals, Adi (Dulquer Salmaan) and Tara (Nithya Menen), who are in a live-in relationship and must navigate their personal aspirations while questioning the traditional concept of love. Mani Ratnam’s direction shines as he captures the complexities of modern-day relationships with subtlety and depth. Dulquer and Nithya bring fresh energy and chemistry, making their characters relatable and endearing. The music by A.R. Rahman is soulful, with songs like “Agaram” and “Mental Manadhil” perfectly complementing the film’s themes. O Kadhal Kanmani is a visually stunning, emotionally engaging film that offers a contemporary take on love and relationships.

9. Velaikkaran 2017




Watch Now

Velaikkaran, directed by Mohan Raja, is a socially conscious action-drama that tackles issues of consumerism, corporate greed, and worker exploitation. Sivakarthikeyan plays Arivu, a young man from the slums who starts working in a big FMCG company and gradually realizes the dark truths behind the glossy brand image. The film stands out for its strong message and impactful dialogues, aiming to create awareness about ethical business practices and social responsibility. Fahadh Faasil delivers a sharp performance as the suave yet manipulative antagonist. While the film is slightly long and leans heavy on messaging, it’s well-intentioned and thought-provoking. Anirudh’s background score and songs like “Karuthavanlaam Galeejaam” add energy. Velaikkaran is a bold, message-driven film that tries to inspire change through cinema.

10. Lover 2018




Watch Now

Lover, directed by Anish Krishna, is a romantic drama that attempts to blend love, emotions, and action. Raj Tarun plays Raj, a carefree, happy-go-lucky guy who falls in love with Charitha, a responsible and kind-hearted nurse played by Riddhi Kumar. The film starts off light and charming, with some breezy romantic moments and songs that set a pleasant tone. However, as the story progresses, it takes a more serious turn with unexpected twists and action elements. Raj Tarun brings energy to his role, and the chemistry between the leads is sweet, though the emotional depth doesn’t always hit hard. Music by Ankit Tiwari and others adds to the romantic mood. Lover is a decent one-time watch for fans of youthful love stories with a bit of action.
